The Sun

The Sun or Sol, is the star at the centre of our solar system responsible for the Earth's climate and weather.

It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core, radiating the energy mainly as visible light and infrared radiation. It is by far the most important source of energy for life on Earth.

Sun facts

  • Age: 4.6 Billion years
  • Type: Yellow Dwarf (G2V)
  • Diameter: 1,392,684 km
  • Equatorial Circumference: 4,370,005.6 km
  • Mass: 1.99 x 10^39 kg (333.060 Earths)
  • Surface Temperature: 5,500 degC
